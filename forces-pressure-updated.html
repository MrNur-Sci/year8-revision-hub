1. Contact Forces

Contact forces happen when objects touch. Resultant force decides whether motion changes.

3. Linear Relationships

A straight line through the origin shows a directly proportional relationship. A spring is only linear up to the elastic limit. After the elastic limit, the graph curves and the deformation becomes inelastic.

Force 30 N
Spring constant5 N/cm
Extension6 cm
Before the elastic limit, force and extension are directly proportional. After the limit, the material may not return to its original shape.

6. Pressure

Pressure = force ÷ area. Smaller area gives greater pressure for the same force.

Force 100 N
Area 0.5 m²
Pressure200 Pa
EquationP = F ÷ A
Rearrange forceF = P × A
Rearrange areaA = F ÷ P
Area must be in m² if pressure is in Pa.

8. Atmospheric Pressure

Air particles collide with surfaces and create pressure. Greater outside pressure gives a larger crushing effect. Water pressure also acts on submerged objects and increases with depth.
