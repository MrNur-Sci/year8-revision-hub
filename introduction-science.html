1. Variables: Exam-Style Scenarios

Learn In exams, variables are usually hidden inside a method. Read the whole practical description, then identify what is changed, what is measured and what is kept the same.

Independent variable

The one thing deliberately changed by the student.

Dependent variable

The result that is measured or recorded.

Control variables

Things kept the same so the test is fair and valid.

Exam sentence frame: “The student changes the ________, measures the ________, and keeps ________ the same.”

3. Lab Safety: Hazard, Risk and Precaution

Learn Hazard, risk and precaution are different.

Hazard

Something that could cause harm.

Risk

The chance of harm happening and how serious it could be.

Precaution

Action taken to reduce the risk.

4. Planning a Fair Test

Learn A good investigation is valid, reliable and accurate.

KeywordMeaningHow to improve it
ValidThe method tests what it is supposed to test.Keep control variables the same.
ReliableRepeats give similar results.Repeat and calculate a mean.
AccurateA measurement is close to the true value.Use suitable equipment and read scales carefully.
PreciseMeasurements are close together or have small intervals.Use equipment with smaller divisions.

6. Conclusions and Evaluations

Learn A strong conclusion uses the trend and evidence. A strong evaluation explains reliability, validity and improvements.

Conclusion builder

1. State the trend/correlation.

2. Use numerical evidence.

3. Link back to the investigation question.

Evaluation builder

1. Identify anomalies or reliability issues.

2. Comment on control variables.

3. Suggest a precise improvement.
