1. Contact Forces

Contact forces happen when objects touch. Resultant force decides whether motion changes.

Balanced?no
If forces are balanced, the object does not change speed. If unbalanced, motion changes.

2. Deformation

Forces can stretch, compress or bend objects. Elastic deformation returns to original shape; plastic deformation does not.

Extension = stretched length - original length. 1 cm = 10 mm.

3. Linear Relationships

A straight line through the origin shows a directly proportional relationship. Force and extension are linear only up to the limit of proportionality.

4–5. Friction and Mini-CFU

Friction opposes motion. Rougher surfaces create more friction. Lubrication and smoother surfaces reduce friction.

Friction acts opposite to the direction of movement.

6. Pressure

Pressure = force ÷ area. Smaller area gives greater pressure for the same force.

Force 100 N
Area 0.5 m²
Pressure200 Pa
EquationP = F ÷ A
Rearrange forceF = P × A
Rearrange areaA = F ÷ P
Area must be in m² if pressure is in Pa.

7. Fluid Pressure

Pressure in a liquid increases with depth because there is more liquid above pushing down.

8. Atmospheric Pressure

Learn Atmospheric pressure is caused by air particles colliding with surfaces. At lower altitude there are more air particles above you, so there are more collisions and pressure is higher. At higher altitude there are fewer air particles above you, so pressure is lower.
